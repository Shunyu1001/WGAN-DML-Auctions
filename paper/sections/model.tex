\section{Auction Model and Target Parameter}

Consider auctions indexed by $j=1,\ldots,n$. Auction $j$ has $N_j$ bidders and
observable characteristics $X_j$. Conditional on $X_j$, bidder valuations
satisfy
\begin{equation}
V_{ij}\mid X_j \overset{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim} F_0(\cdot\mid X_j),
\qquad i=1,\ldots,N_j.
\end{equation}
The baseline observable is the maximum valuation,
\begin{equation}
B_j=\max_{1\leq i\leq N_j}V_{ij}.
\end{equation}
The empirical interpretation of $B_j$ depends on the auction format and the
available data and will be stated explicitly for each application.

For a homogeneous regular valuation distribution, the Myerson reserve price
$r_0$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
1-F_0(r_0)-r_0 f_0(r_0)=0.
\end{equation}
The main theoretical target will be a regularized scalar reserve parameter
$\theta_{0,h}$ defined through a smoothed expected-revenue criterion. The exact
reserve price is recovered as a limiting target when the regularization
parameter $h$ approaches zero under suitable conditions.
