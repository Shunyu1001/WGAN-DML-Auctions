\section{Identification from Order Statistics}

Let $H_0(\cdot\mid N,X)$ denote the conditional distribution of the observed
maximum. Under the baseline assumptions,
\begin{equation}
H_0(b\mid N,X)=F_0(b\mid X)^N.
\end{equation}
For known $N$, the latent distribution is therefore identified by
\begin{equation}
F_0(v\mid X)=H_0(v\mid N,X)^{1/N}.
\end{equation}

This direct inversion supplies both an identification argument and a
nonparametric benchmark. The section will also state which conclusions fail
under unknown competition, correlated bidder values, auction-level unobserved
heterogeneity, endogenous entry, or confusion between the highest bid and the
transaction price.
