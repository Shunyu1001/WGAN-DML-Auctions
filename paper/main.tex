\title{Semiparametric Estimation of Myerson Reserve Prices\\
with WGAN-GP and Double Machine Learning}
\author{Shunyu Hao}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This paper studies estimation and inference for optimal auction reserve prices
when the econometrician observes incomplete bidding data. Under a symmetric
independent-private-values benchmark, the distribution of an observed order
statistic identifies the latent valuation distribution. I use a structural
Wasserstein generative adversarial network with gradient penalty (WGAN-GP) as a
flexible estimator of this infinite-dimensional nuisance object. I then develop
a cross-fitted, Neyman-orthogonal estimator for a regularized Myerson reserve
price. The framework separates distribution learning from inference on the
low-dimensional economic parameter. Monte Carlo experiments compare direct
order-statistic inversion, plug-in WGAN-GP, cross-fitted WGAN-GP, and the
orthogonal WGAN-DML estimator in terms of bias, root mean squared error,
confidence-interval coverage, and revenue regret. The first orthogonal-score
pilot also shows that formal orthogonality is not sufficient when the learned
local tail moments converge too slowly: the second-order remainder can dominate
the nominal influence-function approximation. Oracle and training-fold
empirical ablations restore near-nominal coverage, locating the failure in the
WGAN local nuisance approximation rather than the score construction.
\end{abstract}
