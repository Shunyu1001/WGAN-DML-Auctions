\title{Semiparametric Estimation of Myerson Reserve Prices\\
with WGAN-GP and Double Machine Learning}
\author{Shunyu Hao}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This paper studies estimation and inference for optimal auction reserve prices
when the econometrician observes incomplete bidding data. Under a symmetric
independent-private-values benchmark, the distribution of an observed order
statistic identifies the latent valuation distribution. I use a structural
Wasserstein generative adversarial network with gradient penalty (WGAN-GP) as a
flexible estimator of this infinite-dimensional nuisance object. I then develop
a cross-fitted, Neyman-orthogonal estimator for a regularized Myerson reserve
price and establish fixed-bandwidth asymptotic linearity under local nuisance-
rate conditions. The framework separates distribution learning from inference
on the low-dimensional economic parameter. Monte Carlo experiments compare direct
order-statistic inversion, plug-in WGAN-GP, cross-fitted WGAN-GP, and the
orthogonal WGAN-DML estimator. Formal orthogonality fails when learned local
tail moments converge too slowly. Oracle and training-fold empirical ablations
restore near-nominal coverage, locating the failure in the WGAN nuisance rather
than the score construction. A
shrinking-bandwidth experiment reduces regularization bias but reveals a
second trade-off: aggressive undersmoothing produces high variance, multiple
score roots, and continued undercoverage of the exact reserve. A
covariance-aware Richardson correction substantially improves exact-target
RMSE and coverage on a stable bandwidth path. A three-bandwidth residual-bias
envelope then restores 96.5 percent exact-reserve coverage on that path, while
a union over all root chains shows that omitted roots are not the main source
of failure under aggressive undersmoothing. The fixed-bandwidth regularized
target remains the strongest formally justified inferential claim.
\end{abstract}
