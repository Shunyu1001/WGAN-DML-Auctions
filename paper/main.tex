\title{Semiparametric Estimation of Myerson Reserve Prices\\
with WGAN-GP and Double Machine Learning}
\author{Shunyu Hao}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This paper studies estimation and inference for optimal auction reserve prices
when only an order statistic of bids is observed. Under symmetric independent
private values, its distribution identifies the latent valuation distribution.
I estimate that nuisance object with a structural Wasserstein generative
adversarial network with gradient penalty (WGAN-GP), then develop a cross-fitted
Neyman-orthogonal estimator for a regularized Myerson reserve. For a fixed
bandwidth, I establish asymptotic linearity under local nuisance-rate and root
regularity conditions. Monte Carlo experiments show that good global WGAN fit
and formal orthogonality need not deliver valid inference when learned local
tail moments converge too slowly. Oracle and training-fold empirical
ablations restore near-nominal coverage, locating the failure in the WGAN
nuisance rather than the score construction and motivating a hybrid global
WGAN/local-score procedure. Across bidder counts and baseline, heavy-tail, and
mixture distributions, fixed-bandwidth coverage remains near nominal when the
observed maxima contain local information. It deteriorates under intense
competition when almost no maxima lie near the reserve, revealing a support
limitation that orthogonality cannot repair. Shrinking-bandwidth experiments
further separate regularization bias from sampling uncertainty: a
covariance-aware correction and residual-bias envelope improve inference for
the exact reserve, whereas aggressive undersmoothing creates high variance and
multiple roots. The fixed-bandwidth regularized target remains the paper's
strongest formally justified inferential claim.
\end{abstract}
